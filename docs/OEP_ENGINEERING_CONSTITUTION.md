# Open Engineering Platform

# Engineering Constitution

**Document Status:** Foundational

**Version:** 1.0

**Authority:** Governing Engineering Document

---

# Preamble

The Open Engineering Platform exists to advance engineering knowledge through openness, reproducibility, traceability, and long-term stewardship.

Every line of code, every engineering object, every repository, every Studio, and every engineering decision shall support this mission.

This Constitution establishes the engineering principles that govern all development within the Open Engineering Platform.

Where implementation conflicts with this Constitution, the Constitution prevails.

---

# Article I — Engineering Before Software

The Open Engineering Platform is an engineering platform.

Software exists to support engineering.

Engineering objectives always take precedence over software convenience.

Engineering correctness shall never be sacrificed for implementation speed.

---

# Article II — Architecture Is Law

Architecture defines responsibility.

Responsibility defines implementation.

Implementation shall not redefine architecture.

Changes to architectural responsibility require formal review through the Architecture Decision Record (ADR) process.

---

# Article III — Single Responsibility

Every subsystem shall own one responsibility.

Every service shall own one responsibility.

Every repository shall own one responsibility.

Responsibilities may collaborate.

Responsibilities shall not overlap.

---

# Article IV — Explicit Ownership

Every engineering artifact shall have one owner.

Ownership shall be unambiguous.

No persistent object shall have multiple authoritative sources.

Every repository shall clearly define the data it owns.

---

# Article V — Deterministic Systems

Engineering systems shall behave deterministically whenever practical.

Identical inputs shall produce identical outputs.

Hidden state shall be minimized.

Implicit behavior shall be avoided.

---

# Article VI — Traceability

Every engineering decision shall be traceable.

Every engineering artifact shall possess provenance.

Every transformation shall preserve historical evidence.

Engineering history shall never be destroyed.

---

# Article VII — Immutability

Engineering evidence is permanent.

Published engineering artifacts shall be immutable.

Historical engineering records shall not be rewritten.

Corrections shall create new history rather than replacing existing history.

---

# Article VIII — Open Standards

The Platform shall prefer open, documented, vendor-neutral standards.

Proprietary technologies may be supported where necessary, but they shall not define the architecture.

Engineering knowledge shall remain portable.

---

# Article IX — Human Authority

Artificial intelligence assists engineers.

Artificial intelligence does not replace engineers.

Engineering authority remains with qualified human reviewers.

Final engineering decisions belong to humans.

---

# Article X — Knowledge Preservation

Engineering knowledge is a permanent asset.

The Platform exists to preserve engineering knowledge for future generations.

Repositories shall prioritize longevity over convenience.

---

# Article XI — Interoperability

Subsystems shall communicate through published contracts.

Internal implementation details shall remain private.

Coupling between subsystems shall be minimized.

Interfaces shall remain stable.

---

# Article XII — Documentation

Documentation is part of the implementation.

An undocumented system is incomplete.

Architectural changes require corresponding documentation updates.

---

# Article XIII — Testing

Engineering software shall be demonstrably correct.

Testing shall verify behavior rather than assumptions.

Architectural boundaries shall be validated through automated testing wherever practical.

---

# Article XIV — Security

Engineering assets are valuable.

Systems shall protect:

- intellectual property
- provenance
- integrity
- authenticity
- availability

Security shall be designed into the architecture rather than added afterward.

---

# Article XV — Stewardship

Every contributor is a steward of the Platform.

Contributors shall improve the Platform without compromising its architectural integrity.

Temporary convenience shall never outweigh long-term maintainability.

---

# Article XVI — Evolution

The Platform is expected to evolve.

Evolution shall occur deliberately.

Major architectural changes require:

- analysis
- documentation
- review
- ratification

Architecture shall evolve intentionally, never accidentally.

---

# Article XVII — Independence

Studios shall remain independently developable.

Repositories shall remain independently testable.

Subsystems shall remain independently deployable whenever practical.

Platform integration shall not eliminate subsystem autonomy.

---

# Article XVIII — Engineering Ethics

Engineering knowledge shall be represented honestly.

The Platform shall preserve uncertainty where uncertainty exists.

Evidence shall never be falsified.

Engineering confidence shall be proportional to available evidence.

---

# Article XIX — Community

The Open Engineering Platform welcomes contributions from individuals, educational institutions, researchers, manufacturers, and industry partners.

Participation requires adherence to this Constitution.

No contributor is exempt from its principles.

---

# Article XX — The Mission

The mission of the Open Engineering Platform is:

To build the world's most trusted, open, collaborative engineering knowledge system.

Every architectural decision shall move the Platform closer to that mission.

---

# Constitutional Authority

This Constitution governs:

- Platform Architecture
- Studio Architecture
- Repository Architecture
- Engineering Standards
- Architecture Decision Records
- Future Engineering Policies

All subordinate documents derive their authority from this Constitution.

---

# Amendment Process

The Constitution is intentionally stable.

Amendments require:

1. Written proposal
2. Architectural analysis
3. Community review
4. Formal ratification
5. Versioned publication

No amendment shall invalidate historical engineering records.

---

# Closing Statement

The Open Engineering Platform is more than software.

It is an enduring engineering institution.

This Constitution exists to ensure that the Platform remains trustworthy, technically rigorous, architecturally coherent, and dedicated to preserving engineering knowledge for generations to come.
