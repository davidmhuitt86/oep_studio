# SDD-019

# Engineering Object Philosophy

Version: 1.0

Status: Draft

---

# Purpose

This document defines the philosophical model behind Engineering Objects.

Engineering Objects are the atomic units of engineering knowledge within OEP.

Everything inside an OEP Repository ultimately becomes an Engineering Object.

The goal is to create a universal engineering model that remains valid regardless of engineering discipline.

---

# Engineering Reality

The physical world exists independently of documentation.

A bolt exists whether or not someone has written a service manual.

A wire exists whether or not a schematic has been drawn.

An engineering object therefore represents something that exists in engineering reality, not merely in documentation.

---

# Engineering Objects are Facts

Engineering Objects represent facts.

Examples:

A timing chain exists.

A connector exists.

A CAN message exists.

A torque specification exists.

A procedure exists.

A warning exists.

An image exists.

A measurement exists.

A software function exists.

A document exists.

---

# Relationships Create Meaning

Objects alone have limited value.

Relationships create engineering knowledge.

Example:

Timing Chain

↓

Driven By

↓

Crankshaft

↓

Drives

↓

Camshaft

↓

Timed By

↓

Timing Marks

Knowledge emerges from relationships.

---

# Documents are Evidence

Documents are not engineering truth.

Documents support engineering truth.

Examples:

Factory Service Manual

↓

Supports

↓

Timing Chain Procedure

Engineering truth is represented by Engineering Objects and Relationships.

---

# Engineering Objects are Reusable

Every Engineering Object may participate in unlimited contexts.

Example:

Water Pump

Referenced By:

* Cooling System Repair
* Timing Chain Replacement
* Front Cover Removal
* Exploded Assembly
* Parts Catalog
* Failure Analysis

The object exists once.

The repository reuses it everywhere.

---

# Objects Never Belong to Documents

Documents reference Engineering Objects.

Engineering Objects do not belong to documents.

This prevents duplication.

---

# Objects May Have Many Representations

Example:

Connector C103

Representations:

* Photograph
* Wiring Diagram
* Exploded View
* 3D Model
* Service Manual
* Test Procedure

These are different representations of one Engineering Object.

---

# Engineering Objects are Timeless

Documentation changes.

Engineering knowledge evolves.

The Engineering Object persists.

Its relationships evolve.

Its evidence grows.

Its lifecycle advances.

Its identity remains constant.

---

# Human Knowledge

The repository captures engineering understanding.

Not merely engineering data.

A technician's field observation may become an Engineering Object.

A manufacturer's correction bulletin may become an Engineering Object.

A laboratory measurement may become an Engineering Object.

Engineering knowledge expands continuously.

---

# Discipline Independence

The same Engineering Object model shall support:

Automotive

Electrical

Mechanical

Hydraulic

Pneumatic

Marine

Industrial

Robotics

Aerospace

Medical

Civil

Software

Future engineering domains shall extend the model rather than replace it.

---

# Repository Principle

Repositories do not organize files.

Repositories organize engineering reality.

---

# Engineering Graph

Every Engineering Object participates in a graph.

The graph is not a visualization.

The graph is the engineering model itself.

Visualizations merely display portions of that model.

---

# Design Goal

An engineer should be able to begin with any Engineering Object and explore the complete engineering context surrounding it without caring which document originally introduced that knowledge.

Engineering Objects become the language through which OEP understands engineering itself.
